Harare CBD Traffic Monitoring Solution

A Proposal for ZINARA

Presented by Jedz Intelligent Systems & Drone Solutions

Harare's Urban Mobility Challenge

Effective urban planning and traffic management require accurate, real-time data. Currently, Harare's CBD faces significant congestion, impacting economic activity and commuter time. Our objective is to provide a robust system for monitoring and counting vehicle flow to enable data-driven decision-making.

Estimated Daily Vehicle Composition

This chart illustrates the estimated breakdown of vehicle types entering the CBD daily. Private cars represent the largest segment, highlighting a key area for traffic flow optimization.

Peak Congestion Times

Traffic volume fluctuates significantly, with clear peaks during morning and evening commutes. Any monitoring solution must be capable of handling this high-volume, dynamic environment.

Option 1: Hybrid ANPR & Drone System

This solution combines fixed, ground-level sensors (ANPR) for continuous monitoring with the flexible, wide-area coverage of drones for verification and non-standard vehicle counting.

How It Works

Step 1: Data Capture

Fixed ANPR sensors at key intersections capture license plates & vehicle counts 24/7.

⬇

Step 2: Drone Verification

Drones fly periodic routes to validate sensor counts and capture data on motorcycles, cyclists, and pedestrians.

⬇

Step 3: AI Fusion

Data from both sources is fused and processed by an AI model for a unified, highly accurate traffic count.

Feasibility Profile

The hybrid model shows strong all-around performance, balancing high accuracy and real-time data with good scalability and moderate maintenance costs.

Option 2: AI-Enabled Drone Monitoring

A fully drone-based system leveraging advanced AI for real-time object detection and counting. This offers unparalleled flexibility and the highest data accuracy, ideal for dynamic environments.

How It Works

Step 1: Autonomous Flight

A fleet of drones autonomously patrols pre-defined routes covering the entire CBD, streaming high-resolution video.

⬇

Step 2: Real-time AI Detection

Live video feeds are processed by an AI model (e.g., YOLOv8) to detect, classify (car, bus, truck, moto), and count all objects.

⬇

Step 3: Data Dashboard

Counts and flow maps are updated in real-time on a central dashboard for ZINARA operators.

Feasibility Profile

This option excels in accuracy and real-time data delivery. Its main challenges are weather resilience and the higher operational cost of managing a drone fleet.

Option 3: Satellite & SAR System

This high-level solution uses satellite imagery (Optical and Synthetic Aperture Radar - SAR) combined with AI. It provides macro-level data and is unmatched in scalability and all-weather capability.

How It Works

Step 1: Image Acquisition

High-resolution optical and SAR satellite data is tasked and acquired during key periods.

⬇

Step 2: SAR & AI Processing

SAR data penetrates clouds, ensuring 24/7 all-weather monitoring. AI models detect and classify vehicles from both data sources.

⬇

Step 3: Macro Analysis

Provides city-wide density maps and counts, ideal for large-scale planning rather than real-time intersection control.

Feasibility Profile

This solution's strengths are its immense scalability (covering the entire region) and perfect weather resilience. It trades real-time granularity for macro-level insight.

Comparative Analysis

A direct comparison of the three proposed solutions across the most critical metrics for ZINARA. The choice depends on the primary objective: balanced performance, maximum accuracy, or macro-level scalability.

Solution Metrics Head-to-Head

The AI Drone solution offers the highest accuracy but at a moderate cost and lower scalability. The Hybrid system provides a balanced profile, while the Satellite system excels in scalability at the expense of granular accuracy and real-time data.

Recommendation & Next Steps

Based on the analysis, we recommend the Hybrid ANPR & Drone System solution based on the strategic analysis of continuity requirements, infrastructural constraints, and regulatory limitations, which are critical for active traffic management in a dense CBD environment.

We propose a phased pilot program, starting with key intersections, to validate the system's performance in Harare's specific context. We look forward to discussing this proposal in further detail.
